Securing the Digital Future: The National Security Scheme in Spain

By: MA. Roldán de Haro

The National Security Scheme (ENS) of Spain is a regulatory framework established to guarantee the protection of information managed by public administrations and their suppliers. Its main objective is to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, availability, authenticity and traceability of data. Implemented by Royal Decree 3/2010 and updated by Royal Decree 951/2015, the ENS establishes minimum security measures and controls. It requires organizations to conduct ongoing risk management and take a proactive approach to threats. Adaptation to the ENS is mandatory for all public sector entities and is considered essential for cybersecurity in the government sphere in Spain. Complying with the ENS improves resilience and confidence in the State's digital services.

Flipper Zero: The Multi-Tool Gadget for Ethical Hackers

By: MA. Roldán de Haro

The Flipper Zero is a multifunctional device designed for security research and ethical hacking. Equipped with multiple interfaces, it allows you to interact with a variety of devices and systems, including RFID, NFC, infrared and radio frequency. Compact and easy to transport, it has become a popular tool among cybersecurity enthusiasts and professionals in the sector. With the Flipper Zero, you can clone access cards, analyze radio signals, and perform penetration tests on wireless networks. Its intuitive interface and open source encourage experimentation and customization, allowing users to expand its capabilities according to their needs. In short, Flipper Zero is a powerful tool that promotes computer security and understanding of the technologies around us.
